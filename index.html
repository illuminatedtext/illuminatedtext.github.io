The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock.

by T.S. Eliot

Illuminated Text by Manasvi Vanama and Jareeat Purnava


1 I should have been a pair of ragged claws

2 Scuttling across the floors of silent seas.


3 And the afternoon, the evening, sleeps so peacefully!

4 Smoothed by long fingers,

5 Asleep ... tired ... or it malingers,

6 Stretched on the floor, here beside you and me.

7 Should I, after tea and cakes and ices,

8 Have the strength to force the moment to its crisis?

9 But though I have wept and fasted, wept and prayed,

10 Though I have seen my head (grown slightly bald) brought in upon a platter,

11 I am no prophet — and here’s no great matter;

12 I have seen the moment of my greatness flicker,

13 And I have seen the eternal Footman hold my coat, and snicker,

14 And in short, I was afraid.
